Macro Munchies

Project Overview

Macro Munchies makes finding the best recipes easy without the hassle of scrolling through countless recipes and converting measurements by hand.

Project Goal

To design or responsive web app for recipes that meet the needs of its users & sort the problems They are facing we existing apps.

Tools

Figma

My Role

UX Researcher

UI Designer

Timeline

9 Weeks

My Process

Interviews & User Personas

To kick off this project, I first held interviews with my user group. During this step, I got some great insight from my users, and ultimately their ideas influenced the design decisions moving forward. With the help of these successful interviews, I could put together my user personas.

Project Requirements & User Flow

By this time, I began second-guessing what was a “want” versus a “need” for the app to be successful. As a result, I took out most of my app features by mistake, which made my app bland and I wound up wasting time. Thankfully after consulting with my user group, I realized that these features were needed to accomplish their goals. I then revised my documentation, readded these features, and edited my user flow to match.

Mood Board

For the mood board, I wanted to go with warm and welcoming colors and text to get the user’s attention. I also wanted to make sure that the app’s UI design was more on the simple side to avoid overcrowding the interface and overwhelming the user. Picking the right typefaces and colors in my design was a bit challenging, mainly because I wasn’t sure how to introduce some of the colors in my palette correctly.

Sketching Low-Fidelity Wireframe

The low-fidelity wireframes below show my plans for both my responsive web design and the complete mobile app. At this stage, I felt like my thought process was more cohesive and put together than in previous steps.

High-Fidelity Wireframe

When creating the high-fidelity designs, I began to feel more confident in my design choices and I felt my creativity flowing. I believe that my users’ goals were met and the app feels warm & welcoming as intended.

Mockups

When creating the mockups I enjoyed seeing my hard work pay off. I felt like my designs would not have turned out as well if I had not constantly reached out to my user group for feedback.

Retrospective

In conclusion, I learned how user feedback is crucial to the UX/UI design process. Keeping my user group involved throughout the project helped me stay the user-focused majority of the time. I also learned to be open to change and inspiration at any time because your initial idea may not make the final cut. I will implement these ideals in my future projects so that I may not lose sight of the project goals.
